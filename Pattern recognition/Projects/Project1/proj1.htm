    Objectives:

    Design a decision rule on a synthetic data set with two categories. Assume the probability density is Gaussian.

    Data set used:

    Download synth.tr (the training set) and synth.te (the test set) from the course url.

    (80) Basic requirements:

    Use synth.tr to train your decision rule, and use synth.te to test the decision rule.
    • (10/5) Use maximum likelihood estimation to estimate the parameters of the Gaussian
    • (30/20) Use likelihood ratio to derive your decision rule. Illustrate the decision rule as well as the sample locations (use different symbols for different categories) on the same graph.
    • (15/10) Use discriminant function (try all three cases) to derive your decision rule. Illustrate the three decision rules in the above figure and comment on the difference. (Note that the decision boundary from case III should be the same as the one you derived using likelihood ratio.)
    • (10/10) Try different prior probability distributions and evaluate the performance.
    • (15/15) Evaluate the performance of your decision rule extensively. Some methods include calculation and comparison of the classification accuracy of applying different decision rules on the testing set.
  • (+15/20) Use two-modal Gaussian to model the data set and compare the performance with that using the one-modal.
